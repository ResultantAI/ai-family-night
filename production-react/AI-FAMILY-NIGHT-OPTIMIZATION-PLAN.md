### 7-Day Free Trial Structure

**Research**: 7-day trials outperform 14-day and 30-day in conversion (+5.6%), retention (+6.4%), and revenue (+7.9%). 3-day too short for attachment.

**Current**: We have no trial structure
**Implementation**:

#### Day 0: Parent Value Framing + First Child Win
- [ ] Parent gate asks: child age(s), interests, parent goals
- [ ] Show personalized headline: "A 7-day reading boost for [age 8] like [Alex]"
- [ ] Child first session (same day):
  - Short, easy sequence that feels fun-first
  - Produces 1-2 achievements and first collectible
  - Child-facing celebration screen
  - Parent sees: "Today [Emma] completed 3 missions, unlocked 1 badge"

#### Days 1-3: Build Routine and Collection
- [ ] In-app: Daily nudges "Welcome back! One more challenge to unlock [badge]"
- [ ] Progress bar shows "7-day journey" with checkmarks
- [ ] Email to parent (Day 1): "How to get the most from your trial"
  - 3-min parent tour video
  - Time limit settings
- [ ] Email (Day 3): "See what [child] has achieved" with stats/screenshots

#### Days 4-6: Make Value + Loss Concrete (Endowment Effect)
**Research**: Once child has accumulated content (badges, progress), possibility of losing it increases willingness to pay.

- [ ] In-app banners: "Trial ends in 3 days. Keep [child]'s [12 badges] unlocked"
- [ ] Parent dashboard shows:
  - Skills practiced (e.g., "Fractions, word problems")
  - Time used ("Total 38 min this week")
  - Before/after snapshot ("Struggled with X day 1, completed Y by day 4")
- [ ] Messaging uses loss aversion ethically:
  - "All of [Noah]'s creations will be waiting if you upgrade now"
  - If lockout: "[Noah] can still see their gallery, but new missions require subscription"

#### Day 7: Decision & Fallback
- [ ] Focused paywall when parent next interacts:
  - Personalized: "Keep [Lily]'s math streak going"
  - Default to Annual (savings highlighted)
  - Monthly clearly available
- [ ] If decline: Limited free tier (1 game, capped plays/week)

### Collection/Gallery Mechanics (CRITICAL)

**Research**: Endowment effect - people value things more once they own them. Kids show this from age 3-4. Digital collectibles drive strong attachment.

**Current State**: We have no collection system
**Implementation Priority**: HIGH

#### Make Everything Collectible
- [ ] Every game session awards persistent item:
  - Stickers, badges, cards, pets, map locations
- [ ] Create **Gallery/Collection Screen**:
  - Visually rich
  - Easy for kids to show parents: "Look at my Reading Island map!"
  - Groups by game or week

#### Introduce Ownership Immediately
- [ ] First session child gets:
  - Named avatar or pet they customize
  - First "room/shelf/backpack" for earned items
  - Script: "This is *your* Lab. Every time you explore, you'll add something new"

#### Tie Paywall to Preserving "Owned World"
- [ ] At trial end, frame as:
  - "Keep [Mia]'s world growing"
  - "Their [Robot Lab] and [15 badges] stay safe with subscription"
- [ ] Don't delete gallery if they don't pay:
  - Show it but gate new additions
  - Preserves endowment while using mild loss aversion

#### Signal Progress to Parents
- [ ] In parent summaries:
  - "In one week, [Ethan] built collection of 9 science experiments, 4 story badges"
  - Turns abstract learning into visible assets

#### Ethical Boundaries
- âŒ Don't: "All [Zoe]'s work will be deleted if you don't subscribe"
- âŒ Don't: "Tell your parents to pay so we don't delete your pet"
- âœ… Do: Make clear to *parents* decision preserves what child cares about
- âœ… Do: Offer export (download creations even without subscribing)

## Phase 2: Retention & Weekly Ritual Design (Week 3-4)

### Goal: Become a Family Ritual, Not Just a Tool

**Research**: Barbara Fiese - Family rituals have symbolic meaning beyond routines. Apps must embed in daily/weekly flows (bedtime, Sunday planning) and carry emotional significance.

### Core Weekly Structure

**Target**: 5-15 minutes together, 3-4 times per week
**Primary Anchors**: Bedtime weeknights, Sunday planning

#### Weekly Engagement Calendar

**MONDAY â€“ "New Mission + Micro-Win"**
- **Content drop (morning, in-app)**:
  - New weekly theme ("Space Explorers Week")
  - 3-part mission arc for week (Mon/Wed/Sun)
- **Parent push (4-6pm)**:
  - "This week's 10-min family mission: 'Space Explorers Part 1'. Try after dinner."
- **Session (evening, 5-15 min)**:
  - Short co-play activity
  - Fills 1 of 3 mission segments (open loop remains)
  - Child's collection gets one item
  - Prompt to choose Wed session time

**TUESDAY â€“ "Autonomy Day"**
- **No push** (low-pressure)
- **In-app (optional)**:
  - Kids explore related content
  - Variable reward: surprise micro-activity or flashback
  - "Remember when you did [activity] last month?"

**WEDNESDAY â€“ "Skill & Progress Checkpoint"**
- **Parent push (4-6pm)**:
  - "Part 2 unlocked: 10-min 'Word Lab' for this week's theme"
- **Session (evening)**:
  - Activity emphasizing competence (creative challenge, quiz)
  - Visible progress map update (spaceship to next planet)
  - Collection gets another artifact
  - Micro-reflection: "Ask your child: What was trickiest? What are you proud of?"

**THURSDAY â€“ "Memory & Social Proof"**
- **Parent email (morning/midday)**: Weekly recap
  - Time spent, completions, 1-2 highlights
  - "Tonight, ask your child to show favorite thing they created"
- **In-app (optional)**: Flashback to past week's creation

**FRIDAY â€“ "Weekend Teaser + Open Loop"**
- **Parent push (3-5pm)**:
  - "You're 2/3 through this week's mission. Finish Sunday to unlock [badge/page]. 5-10 min."
- **Dashboard shows**: "2 of 3 steps done" + Sunday mission preview

**SATURDAY â€“ "Free Play"**
- **No notifications**
- **In-app (kid-led)**:
  - Browse gallery, finish series
  - Variable reward: Random "Discovery Card" after exploration

**SUNDAY â€“ "Family Ritual + Weekly Reset"**
- **Parent email (early afternoon)**: Weekly recap
  - Time together, accomplishments, highlight screenshots
  - SDT feedback: "You helped your child practice X and Y"
  - Choose next week's theme (autonomy)
- **Parent push (1-2hrs before ritual)**:
  - "Tonight: Part 3 - 10-min 'Family Story & Gratitude'"
- **Session (evening)**:
  - Story, collaborative activity, or gratitude prompt
  - Capture photo/audio/text answering weekly question
  - Complete 3/3 mission segments
  - Add page to "Family Yearbook" (Tinybeans-style memory)
  - Show competence badge ("4 family sessions this month")
  - Flash-forward: "Next week choose new theme together"

### Notification Strategy

**Parent (default)**:
- Mon: 1 push (new mission)
- Wed: 1 push (Part 2)
- Fri: 1 push (weekend teaser)
- Sun: 1 push (ritual reminder) + 1 weekly email

**Optional opt-in**: Daily nudge at chosen time

**Kids (COPPA-compliant)**:
- No direct push without parental consent
- Use in-app "coach character" prompts
- Parent-mediated: "[Child] unlocked new card. Open together."

- [ ] **Named, recurring slots**: "Sunday Story Quest," "Wednesday Word Lab"
- [ ] **Scheduled content drops** at fixed times
- [ ] **Family-set assignments**: "Finish this week's collection"

#### 3. Social Proof Loops
- [ ] **Shared family feed**:
  - Parents/relatives react and comment
  - Weekly email recaps keep everyone in loop
- [ ] **Parent dashboards**:
  - Show reading time, progress
  - Adults praise â†’ child effort loop

  - Friends news feed showing accomplishments (+19% retention boost)

## Phase 3: UX & Design for Ages 5-12 (Week 5-6)

### Motor Control by Age

**Research**: Fine motor precision continues refining until age 10. "Fat-finger" problem is barrier to entry.

#### Age 5-7 ("Mash Phase")
- [ ] Button size: **2cm x 2cm** (76x76px)
- [ ] Padding: 10-15mm safe zones between buttons
- [ ] Single-tap only (no pinch-to-zoom, rotations)
- [ ] Avoid swipe for navigation - use tappable arrows

#### Age 8-10 ("Refinement Phase")
- [ ] Button size: **1.5cm x 1.5cm** (57x57px)
- [ ] Drag-and-drop with **magnetic drop zones** (snap-to-grid)
- [ ] Drop precision lower than pickup precision

#### Age 11-12 ("Adult-Adjacent")
- [ ] Button size: **1cm x 1cm** (44-48px)
- [ ] Complex gestures OK (swipe, pinch)
- [ ] Critical actions still large to prevent errors

### Cognitive Load

**Research**: Kids have high processing speed but limited working memory buffer.

#### Attention Span Metrics
| Age | Sustained Attention | Design Implication |
|-----|---------------------|-------------------|
| 5-7 | 12-18 minutes | 3-5 minute micro-episodes. Auto-save frequent. |
| 8-10 | 20-30 minutes | Break points every 10 minutes. |
| 11-12 | 25-35+ minutes | Can handle nested menus, inventory. |

#### Latency Tolerance
- [ ] **0.1s**: Button feedback (haptic/visual). No delay = "broken"
- [ ] **1.0s**: Transition limit. Longer needs "busy" animation (character dancing, not spinner)
- [ ] **10s**: Absolute loading limit before abandonment

#### Visual Hierarchy
- [ ] Ages 5-7: Never more than **3 primary choices** at once
- [ ] Primary CTA must be visually dominant (larger, brighter, animated)
- [ ] Instructions must be "shown not told" (diegetic tutorials, not modals)

### Bridging Literacy Gaps

**Research**: 5-year-old is pre-literate; 12-year-old fluent. Creative expression shouldn't be gated by reading.

#### Multimodal Input Strategies

**1. Voice-to-Text (VTT)**
- [ ] Always available but never forced
- [ ] Use Entity Extraction AI (keywords vs. perfect sentences)
- [ ] Example: Child draws character, "tells" app name/backstory
- [ ] App transcribes to storybook format

**2. Pre-Written Option Banks ("Mad Libs Pattern")**
- [ ] Sentence starters: "Once upon a time, there was a..."
- [ ] Visual/textual options: [princess icon] [dragon icon] [castle icon]
- [ ] Reinforces literacy (word + image)
- [ ] Reduces spelling anxiety

**3. Emoji and Semiotic Assembly**

## Phase 4: Viral Growth & Sharing (Week 7-8)

### The Psychology Shift: Pride â†’ Awe

**Research**: Modern parents motivated less by "My kid is smart" (pride/bragging) and more by "My kid is creative/kind" (awe/discovery).

**Key Insight**: Awe-based content is less "sharenting" stigma, more genuine relatable experience.

### The "Proud Parent Moment" Matrix 2026

| Dimension | OLD Trigger | NEW Trigger |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Focus** | High Score / Gold Star | Creative Artifact / Process |
| **Nature** | Standardized Certificate | Personalized "Awe" Moment |
| **Channel** | Public Feed Post | Private Family Group |
| **Signal** | "My kid is smart" | "My kid is kind/creative" |

### Shareable Asset Design

#### Platform-Specific Formats

**TikTok & Instagram Reels (9:16, 1080x1920px)**:
- [ ] "Reaction Video" format
  - Record child's voice/reaction during activity
  - Shows *process* not just result
  - Demonstrates progress without feeling staged
- [ ] "Lo-fi" authentic style (not polished/commercial)
- [ ] UGC aesthetic (shaky camera, natural lighting)

**Instagram Stories (9:16, ephemeral)**:
- [ ] Interactive stickers (Polls, Q&A)
  - "Guess what [Child] built today?" with poll
  - Increases engagement, boosts algorithmic visibility
- [ ] Lower barrier to share (disappears in 24hrs)

**Facebook Feed (4:5, 1080x1350px)**:
- [ ] "Then vs. Now" formats (nostalgia)
- [ ] Shows child's progress over month/year
- [ ] Archival motivation for extended family

**iMessage (iOS 26) - HIGHEST CONVERSION**:
- [ ] **Rich Links**: Dynamic og:image showing *their* child's creation
- [ ] **Sticker App**: Allow parent to "peel and stick" earned stickers into family chat
- [ ] **Send with Effect**: Use trigger phrases:
  - "Congratulations" or "Great job" triggers confetti/balloons
- [ ] **Custom Backgrounds**: Generated themed backgrounds from child's work

#### Visual Style for 2026
**Research**: "Instagram-perfect" is OUT. "Middle ground parenting" is IN.

- [ ] Don't look like corporate brochures
- [ ] Look like parent-made or genuine moment capture
- [ ] Handwriting fonts
- [ ] "Scrapbook" aesthetics
- [ ] Allow "messy" context (living room background)
- [ ] Signals authenticity & "support beats aesthetics"

### Referral Program Design

**Research**: Non-cash perks convert ~25% better than pure cash. Value perceived in utility/status.

#### Recommended Incentive: "Scholarship Model"

**Mechanic**: "Gift 3 months of AI Family Night Premium to a friend's child"

**Psychology**:
- Positions referrer as benefactor (high status/generosity)
- Not bargain hunter
- Leverages "support beats aesthetics" trend
- Friends showing up with help > perfect parties

**Alternative Structures**:
| Type | Example | Best For |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Two-Sided Credit** | "Give $20, Get $20" | High-ticket subscriptions |
| **Gamified Milestone** | "Refer 5 to unlock level" | Freemium games |
| **Charity** | "Donate $10 to schools" | Mission-driven apps (6-11% participation but deep loyalty) |
| **Feature Unlock** | "Unlock ad-free" | Utilities, parental controls |

**Research**: Parents hyper-vigilant against spam in Facebook Groups, PTAs, Nextdoor.

#### "Anti-Spam" Protocol

**1. Resource, Not Product Approach**
- âŒ Don't: Post "Download my App" link
- âœ… Do: Share result/resource generated by app
- Example:
  ```
  "My 7-year-old just built this 'Happy Birthday' animation
  ```

- Contact admins before posting
- Offer "Group Exclusive" benefit:
  - "I can give 50 free premium codes to this group"

**3. Nextdoor "Neighbor First" Strategy**
- Use Business profile but engage as neighbor
- Share "Safety Tips" or "Educational Resources"
- Local authenticity critical

## Success Metrics to Track
